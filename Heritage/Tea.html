Tea Estate

Sri Lanka, formerly Ceylon, is a captivating island nation in the Indian Ocean. Known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean," it boasts breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and warm hospitality. Its world-famous tea industry, introduced by the British in the 19th century, has made Sri Lanka the fourth largest tea producer globally. Tea plantations have shaped the economy and culture, providing jobs, stimulating rural growth, and preserving the country's natural beauty. They represent Sri Lanka's resilience and craftsmanship, offering visitors an enchanting journey into the aromatic world of tea and dasha cherished heritage that defines this remarkable island nation. Kandy Tea Plantation Located in the lush hills surrounding the historic city of Kandy, this tea plantation is a treasure trove of sensory delights. Kandy's tea gardens are known for producing robust and aromatic teas that carry a distinct character. Explore the sprawling tea estates, surrounded by verdant hills and shimmering waterfalls, offering a feast for the eyes.
